RiseUp
A smart morning routine system, designed to help families organize their mornings.
The challenge
Scenario Explorations and Personas
Current smart technologies are mainly designed to help users control devices, while individuals actually strive for control over their lives. Achieving this requires a deep understanding of users unique nuances and circumstances which was pursued through a data-enabled design approach and careful user testing in real-life family settings throughout the project.
To scope the project, I explored how personalized smart home solutions could impact the lives of a fictional characters living in 2040. Scenario building and literature research identified morning routines as a promising area of intervention, given their influence on mental well-being and individuals ability to focus throughout the day. Furthermore, coordinating morning routines within shared environments presents additional complexity due to conflicting schedules and family dynamics.

Through data enabled design approach, I decided to finalize the type of data the system would present according to the results of my second user tests.
I conducted an initial user test with an exploratory goal: to understand the use case of RiseUp by creating a creative space with users.
To test the concept within the target group and validate its quality, I planned a second user test involving families. The goal of this test was to understand if, and how, a system that collects and presents data about morning routines, alerts users when routines deviate and offers data analysis and visualizations, can influence users mornings. The first stage was dedicated to understanding morning routines of participants through data gathering with diary entries. Diary entries of the families were gathered through telegram using the data foundry bot.
Analysing User Insights & Visualizing Data
In Situ Validation - Digital Mockup
Data Analysis

To take a step back and understand the various circumstances that the smart system would need to adapt to, I made scenarios on how a routine planner can work in a family environment and in times of disruptions.
Such smart system that is used by multiple people needed to account for a wide range of factors. I created a simple flow and low fidelity prototypes to study the data flow. I decided on core functionalities that such a smart system would need to accommodate which were data gathering from devices inside the house, creating the routine and alerting individuals when disruptions/ conflicts occur.
This setup was intended to encourage participants to step outside their personal routines and collaboratively build a shared sense of family during the session. I ran a within-subject study, having each participant experienced routine disruptions both with and without RiseUp. I observed participants actions, specifically how they communicated with each other.
I designed a more detailed data flow to explore how the data gathered by the smart home system can be used in presenting insights for users to manage their mornings in a more efficient way. Visualizing the interface was a helpful method to explore how RiseUp could support users in making more informed and mindful decisions about their routines.
After the data collection, I visualised the data to discuss in individual interviews. As participants reflected on their routines, many noticed details they hadnt paid attention to before. This insight was brought back into the design process and resulted in a feature that presents data analysis for the users to reflect on. The visualizations would show deviations from their planned routines, skipped activities, and possible suggestions to support further reflection.
In the second stage of the user test, participants were given access to an interactive digital prototype that visualized their morning routines. They engaged with the prototype by adjusting it to better fit their schedules. the system then generated a suggested routine that balanced their current and desired routines. At the end of each day, participants received a routine analysis summarizing their activities.
It became clear that participants have high expectations for personalization, for it to know more than the actions they are taking in the moment. For the system to make such personalized suggestions, it must also take into account users past behaviour, recurring patterns, external factors and the correlation between routines of others. This required a machine learning algorithm that was included in the final prototype

Bathbobber
An accessible and efficient way of tracking ice bath sessions.
The challange
Planning
Bathbobber, a smart ice bath timer/thermometer , aims to provide an accessible way of tracking ice bath sessions or cold plunges. My design challenge was exploring and developing the interaction system of BathBobber, focusing on the data that will be communicated and how. I aimed to identify areas for improvement in the current design and the interaction, proposing solutions
Because I worked on the project independently, I took on the role of product owner, managing the timeline and the considerations surrounding it. This approach helped structure communication with the client and balance my personal growth goals with the clients business objectives.
I created elaborated scenarios to explore the interaction, see areas for improvement in user experience. I included personas with different characteristics, occupations, financial status, goals and values as these can influence their behavior towards the activity.

Risk Analysis
System Design
To define the structure of the system, I developed a comprehensive UI flow that mapped the possible states of the interface and the different interaction pathways available to the user. This system design outlined the underlying logic of the interface, clarifying how the system responds to user inputs and how different components and interactions connect within the service.
Based on the defined system flow, I created UI visualisations to explore how these interactions would be presented to the user. These visualisations focused on clarity and intuitiveness, helping to communicate the interaction design and interface concept to both users and the client.
